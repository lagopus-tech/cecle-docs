# Screen Printing Machine Selection Guide and Product Overview

## I. What is a Screen Printing Machine?

A screen printing machine is a classic piece of printing equipment that uses a screen printing plate and applies pressure through a squeegee to force ink through the mesh openings onto a substrate.

*   **Core Value： It delivers a thick ink layer with full, vibrant colors, and offers exceptional adaptability to various substrate materials and shapes (whether flat, curved, rigid, or soft).

*   **Applicable Industries： Widely used in packaging boxes, electronic products (printed circuit boards, membrane switches), advertising signs, garment printing (T-shirts, non-woven bags), glass, etc.

###Core Workflow Introduction

Screen printing is a systematic process. Beyond the main machinery, it requires close coordination with pre-press, post-press steps, and auxiliary materials.

1. Pre-press Preparation & Machine Tuning (Pre-process)

Screen making： Fabricating a screen plate coated with photosensitive emulsion based on the design pattern. The mesh openings in the design area remain open, while the non-pattern areas are completely sealed.

Ink Mixing： Selecting the appropriate ink (such as UV ink or solvent-based ink) according to the substrate material, and adding a thinner to adjust its viscosity.

Registration： Fixing the screen onto the machine's screen frame and adjusting the X/Y/Z axes as well as the off-contact distance to ensure identical printing positions every time.

2. Printing Execution (Core Process)

Once the equipment starts, ink is poured onto the screen.

**The flood bar first spreads the ink evenly across the surface of the screen.

**The squeegee then presses down and scrapes across the screen at a preset pressure and speed, forcing the ink through the mesh openings onto the product surface to complete the pattern transfer.
*   **Representative Models:** `TD-1525`、`TD-2533`、`TD-3050`, etc.

3. Curing & Cleaning (Post-process)

Drying/Curing：Printed products must be air-dried, baked, or quickly cured using a UV curing machine.
*   **Representative Models: ** `TD-UV400/TD-UV600`

Screen Plate Cleaning：Thoroughly cleaning residual ink from the screen plate using screen wash to prevent blockages and prepare for its next use.

###Product Naming Rules

**Based on the machine's power form: **

*   **MS / (A)MS **  = Manual (Manual Series)

*   **TD** = T&D Standard Power Models (Pneumatic/Electric Series)

*   **TD-R** =  Roll to Roll (Fully Automatic Flatbed Roll to Roll Series)

*   **TD-UV** = UV Curing (UV Ultraviolet Curing Auxiliary Equipment)

**Based on the machine's numerical suffix: **

The numerical suffix for flatbed machines usually indicates the maximum printing area. For example, TD-2533 represents a maximum printing area of 250×330mm, while TD-60100 represents a maximum printing area of 600×1000mm.

## III. Technical FAQ & Common Troubleshooting

### 1. Model Selection Advice

*   **Q1：How do I choose the right screen printing machine? **
 *   **A：** Step 1: Look at the substrate shape to determine the broad category (whether you need to print flat or curved surfaces). Step 2: Check the printing area to determine the specific model (based on your product size and artwork dimensions). Step 3: Factor in your budget and required production capacity.

### 2. Tuning and Printing Effects

*   **Q2：Why does the printed pattern have blurry edges, jagged burrs, or bleeding ink?**
*   **A：** This is usually caused by the following factors:
    1.**Too much thinner added：** The ink is too thin, resulting in excessive fluidity. You will need to remix ink with a higher viscosity.
    2.**Off-contact distance too short：** The screen plate sits too close to the product, preventing the screen from snapping back immediately after the squeegee passes, causing sticking.
    3.**Excessive squeegee pressure or incorrect angle： **This deforms the squeegee blade and forces out excess ink. 

*   **Q3：Why is part of the printed pattern missing or showing broken lines?**
*   **A：** The most likely culprit is a blocked mesh. Impurities or rapid-drying ink can clog the mesh openings. You should immediately wipe the underside of the screen plate with screen wash. Additionally, for curved surface printing, check whether the fixture secures the product entirely without any slipping or loosening.

### 3. Maintenance and Care

*   **Q4：What must be done every day before clocking out?**
*   **A：**  The single most critical rule: you must thoroughly clean the screen! If ink (especially UV ink or inks mixed with curing agents) dries inside the mesh openings, the screen will be permanently ruined. Remove the screen plate and rinse it with dedicated screen wash until the mesh holes are completely clear and light can pass through. At the same time, clean off the edges of the squeegee and flood bar.

*   **Q5：Do pneumatic screen printing machines require lubrication? **
*   **A： **Yes. Regularly inspect the oil-water separator (F.R.L. unit) at the back of the machine. Drain accumulated water from the water cup in a timely manner, and fill the oil cup with an appropriate amount of specialized pneumatic lubricant to ensure the service life of internal air cylinders and solenoid valves.
