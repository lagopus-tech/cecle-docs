# Screen Printing Machine Selection Guide & Product Overview

## I. What is a Screen Printing Machine?

A screen printing machine (Screen Printing Machine) is a classic printing device that uses a screen printing plate and the pressure of a squeegee to force ink through the mesh holes onto the substrate.

*   **Core Value：** Thick ink layer, rich and vibrant colors; highly adaptable to various substrate materials and shapes (flat, curved, rigid, or flexible surfaces).

*   **Applicable Industries：** Widely used in metal plates, electronic products (printed circuit boards, membrane switches), advertising signage, garment printing (T-shirts, non-woven bags), glass, etc.

### Core Workflow Introduction

Screen printing is a systematic engineering process. Besides the main machine, it requires close cooperation with pre-press and post-press processes, as well as auxiliary materials.

1. Pre-press Preparation and Machine Adjustment (Pre-process)

Screen making： Make a screen plate with photosensitive emulsion based on the design pattern. The pattern area remains open/permeable, while the non-pattern area is sealed off.

Ink Mixing： Choose the appropriate ink (such as UV ink or solvent ink) based on the substrate material, and add Thinner to adjust the viscosity.

Registration/Alignment： Fix the screen on the machine's screen frame, and adjust the X/Y/Z axes and the off-contact distance (Net distance) to ensure absolute consistency for every print position.

2. Printing Execution (Core Process)

After starting the equipment, ink is poured onto the screen.

**The Flood bar first spreads the ink evenly across the screen surface.**

**The Squeegee presses down and scrapes across the screen at a set pressure and speed, squeezing the ink through the mesh onto the product surface to complete the pattern transfer.**
*   **Representative Models:** `TD-1525`、`TD-2533`、`TD-3050`, etc.

3. Curing & Cleaning (Post-process)

Drying/Curing：The printed products need to be air-dried, baked, or quickly cured through a UV curing machine.
*   **Representative Models: ** `TD-UV400/TD-UV600`

Screen Cleaning：Use Screen Wash to thoroughly clean any residual ink on the screen for the next use and to prevent screen clogging.

## III. Technical FAQ & Common Troubleshooting

### 1. Model Selection Advice

*   **Q1：How do I choose the right screen printing machine? **
 *   **A：** Step 1: Look at the shape of the substrate to determine the main category (whether you need to print on a flat or curved surface); Step 2: Look at the printing area to determine the specific model (based on product size and pattern size); Step 3: Look at budget and production capacity (depending on personal budget and requirements).

### 2. About Machine Adjustment and Printing Results

*   **Q2：Why are the printed patterns blurry at the edges, fuzzy, or bleeding ink?**
*   **A：** This is usually caused by the following reasons:
    1.**Too much thinner added：** The ink is too thin, resulting in excessive fluidity. The ink needs to be remixed to a higher viscosity.
    2.**Off-contact distance too small：**The screen and the product are too close. The screen does not instantly snap back after the squeegee passes, causing sticking.
    3.**Squeegee pressure too high or angle incorrect： **This causes the squeegee to deform, squeezing out excess ink.

*   **Q3：Why is the printed pattern missing a piece or showing broken lines?**
*   **A：** The most likely cause is screen clogging. Impurities in the ink or ink that dries too quickly have blocked the mesh holes. Immediately wipe the bottom of the screen with Screen Wash. Additionally, if it is curved printing, check whether the fixture fixes the product completely without any looseness or slippage.

### 3. Maintenance and Care

*   **Q4：What must be done every day before leaving work?**
*   **A：**  The most important point: the screen must be thoroughly cleaned! If ink (especially UV ink or ink with a curing agent) dries inside the mesh holes, the screen will be completely ruined. Please remove the screen and wash it with specialized screen wash until the mesh holes are transparent to light. At the same time, clean the edges of the squeegee and flood bar.

*   **Q5：Do pneumatic screen printing machines need lubricating oil?**
*   **A： **Yes. Please regularly check the oil-water separator (FRL unit) at the back of the machine, drain the water from the filter cup in time, and add an appropriate amount of specialized pneumatic lubricating oil to the oil cup to ensure the service life of the internal air cylinders and solenoid valves.

## IV. Manufacturer Customization Capabilities & Auxiliary Ecosystem

As a professional manufacturer, we not only provide standard models but also support a complete screen printing ecosystem:

1.  **Product Fixtures：** For some products, custom fixtures are required. We provide fixture manufacturing; you need to send samples to us, and we will build the fixtures based on the samples to secure the products.

2. **Voltage and Frequency Customization：**Standard models are mostly 220V/50HZ or 380V. For markets such as North America and Central/South America, we can customize 110V/60HZ single-phase or 220V/60HZ three-phase electrical systems (free or paid).

3. **Vacuum Suction Table Optimization：**  For extremely thin paper and PVC films, we can enhance the suction of the table's vacuum generator to prevent the "screen-lifting" displacement phenomenon.

4. **UV Curing Ecosystem Interfacing：**   We provide UV curing machines such as TD-UV400 / TD-UV600. If clients use UV ink to print on phone cases, circuit boards, etc., the screen printing machine can output directly to the UV tunnel oven, achieving absolute drying of the ink within seconds and significantly boosting production line efficiency.
