# Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) Machine Selection Guide & Product Overview

Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) replaces the air inside the packaging with specific preservation gases (such as a mixture of nitrogen, carbon dioxide, and oxygen), effectively inhibiting microbial growth, extending the shelf life of food, and locking in its original color and taste[cite: 1].

To meet the needs of enterprises of different scales, we offer three core machine models: **Drawer Type**, **Rotary Type**, and **Continuous Type**[cite: 1].

## II. Detailed Differences Between Styles

### 1. Drawer Type - The Choice for Flexibility
* **Operating Mode**: Features a drawer-style design where the operator manually pushes the tray with materials into the sealing chamber and pulls it out after packaging[cite: 1].
* **Advantages**:
    * **Cost-Effective**: The preferred choice for entry-level investment[cite: 1].
    * **Space-Friendly**: Extremely small footprint, suitable for retail stores or back-of-house operations with limited space[cite: 1].
    * **Quick Mold Change**: Suitable for multi-spec, small-batch production; mold changes take only a few minutes[cite: 1].
* **Applicable Scenarios**: Braised food chain stores, laboratory research, start-up fresh food e-commerce[cite: 1].

### 2. Rotary Type - Balance of Efficiency and Space
* **Operating Mode**: Utilizes a rotary turntable to achieve dual-station synchronous operation[cite: 1]. While the machine is sealing on one side, the operator can place the next batch of trays on the other, eliminating waiting time[cite: 1].
* **Advantages**:
    * **Uninterrupted Operation**: Sealing and loading are performed simultaneously, doubling the output compared to the Drawer Type[cite: 1].
    * **Operational Safety**: The loading area is physically isolated from the internal sealing area, ensuring operator safety[cite: 1].
    * **High Stability**: Suitable for medium-sized factories with specific production speed requirements[cite: 1].
* **Applicable Scenarios**: Medium-sized cooked food processing centers, cold-chain fresh-cut vegetable suppliers, chain meal distribution centers[cite: 1].

### 3. Continuous Type - The Industrial Benchmark for Efficiency
* **Operating Mode**: Full-automatic assembly line operation[cite: 1]. Trays enter the sealing chamber via a conveyor belt/chain, and are automatically discharged after vacuuming, gas flushing, and sealing[cite: 1].
* **Advantages**:
    * **Extremely High Output**: Multi-cavity molds combined with continuous feeding achieve large-scale mass production[cite: 1].
    * **Labor Saving**: Can be directly integrated with upstream automatic weighing/filling and downstream labeling/coding lines, significantly reducing labor costs[cite: 1].
    * **Servo-Driven**: Extremely precise positioning with uniform sealing pressure, resulting in high aesthetic quality[cite: 1].
* **Applicable Scenarios**: Large ready-made meal production bases, large-scale meat processing plants, food export enterprises[cite: 1].

## III. Selection Decision: Which Model is Best for You?

Choose according to your core business needs[cite: 1]:

1. **Based on Production Budget (Output Focus)**:
    * **Daily Output < 2,000 trays**: Choose **Drawer Type (MAP-2)**[cite: 1].
    * **Daily Output 2,000 - 6,000 trays**: Choose **Rotary Type (RMAP-4634)**[cite: 1].
    * **Daily Output > 6,000 trays**: Choose **Continuous Type (AMAP-5827)**[cite: 1].

2. **Based on Site Conditions (Space Constraints)**:
    * Extremely narrow space or retail store use: **Drawer Type** is the only choice[cite: 1].
    * Have a formal production workshop and pursue automation: Prioritize **Continuous Type**[cite: 1].

3. **Based on Product Variety**:
    * If tray shapes are complex and frequently changing, **Drawer Type** or **Rotary Type** offer more convenient mold changes[cite: 1].
