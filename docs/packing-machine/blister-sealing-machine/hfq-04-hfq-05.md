> **Core Abstract:**
> The HFQ-04 and HFQ-05 series are fully automatic turntable high-frequency blister welding machines, ideal for large-scale packaging factories, electronic accessory manufacturers, and medical blister plants. These machines excel in non-stop, high-speed thermal bonding for APET, PETG, PVC, and leather materials without requiring blister oil. The core advantage lies in their advanced PLC touch screen control and 3-station automatic rotary worktable, which significantly boosts production efficiency up to 6-10 pcs/min. While the basic HFQ-04 provides reliable automatic rotation and heat sealing, the upgraded HFQ-05 features a servo robotic arm for fully unattended automatic unloading, perfectly integrating into 24-hour continuous assembly lines to reduce labor costs.

## 1. Product Overview
HFQ-04 and HFQ-05 are fully automatic turntable high-frequency thermal bonding machines. They are equipped with an integrated high-frequency oscillation system and PLC touch screen automatic control system, together with a 3-station automatic rotary worktable. The machines are suitable for continuous packaging of APET, PETG, GAG, PVC and PP double blisters, blister-card packs, toothbrushes and small hardware. They can also thermally fuse EVA, leather, PU and OPP flexible materials and can be connected to automated production lines.

The two models share identical base configurations, dimensions and power ratings. The core difference lies in material handling: HFQ-04 is a basic automatic model with manual loading and unloading, while HFQ-05 is upgraded with a servo robotic arm to automatically pick and place finished goods for unattended continuous production. The machine weighs 500KG (1102.31lbs), fitted with a 200×350mm heating platen and a maximum processing size of 350×200mm. Custom molds with 1 to 4 cavities are supported, with precise temperature adjustment ranging from 0℃ to 300℃, ideal for mass non-stop production in medium and large packaging factories.

## 4. Operation Process
### HFQ-04 Basic Automatic Operation Steps
1. Install identical custom molds on all three turntable stations, connect the 0.6~0.8MPa air compressor and power supply.
2. Set high-frequency power, sealing temperature, turntable speed and holding time on the touch screen, then save the production program.
3. Preheat the high-frequency generator and heating platen, run empty automatic cyclic test to inspect spark protection and pressing performance.
4. Manually place blisters, paper cards and leather workpieces into positioning slots on the outer feeding station.
5. The machine automatically rotates the station to the heat sealing zone for constant-temperature high-frequency fusion, then rotates to the unloading station after completion.
6. Manually take out finished goods and continuously feed raw materials for uninterrupted cyclic production.
7. Turn off high-frequency system, heating system and air supply after production. Clean plastic residues on molds after full cooling and maintain the turntable indexer regularly.

### HFQ-05 Robotic Arm Automatic Operation Steps
1. Install custom molds on turntable stations, connect air source and power supply, and set robotic arm stroke & speed parameters on the touch screen synchronously.
2. Adjust high-frequency power, temperature and turntable cycle, then save the integrated full-automatic operation program.
3. Preheat the machine and run empty full-automatic cycle to test the linkage of turntable, pressing and robotic picking movements.
4. Only manually feed raw workpieces on the feeding station; no manual unloading is required.
5. The turntable automatically transports workpieces to the heat sealing station for high-frequency fusion. After rotating to the unloading position, the servo robotic arm grabs finished products automatically.
6. Can connect to conveyor lines to deliver finished goods to the next process, supporting 24-hour unattended cyclic mass production.
7. Shut down high-frequency and heating systems after work. Disassemble and clean molds after cooling; lubricate robotic guide rails and turntable bearings periodically.

### FAQs
Q: Do both machines require an air compressor?
A: Yes. Both adopt pneumatic pressing structure; a stable air source of 0.6~0.8MPa is mandatory for normal operation.

Q: Are molds interchangeable between HFQ-04 and HFQ-05?
A: Yes. They share the same heating platen size, so matched molds can be used interchangeably.

Q: Is blister oil needed for blister-card packaging?
A: No. The built-in high-frequency oscillator realizes material molecular self-heating fusion without blister oil, delivering stronger sealing strength.

Q: Can the robotic arm be retrofitted to HFQ-04 later?
A: The robotic arm is an exclusive integrated structure for HFQ-05 and cannot be installed on HFQ-04 after delivery.

Q: How long is the machine warranty?
A: Uniform 1-year whole machine warranty excluding consumable parts; lifelong technical support is available.
