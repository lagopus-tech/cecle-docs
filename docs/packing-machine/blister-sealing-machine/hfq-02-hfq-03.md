## 1. Product Overview
HFQ-02 and HFQ-03 belong to the same series of desktop manual high-frequency thermal bonding equipment, sharing identical heating, pressurization and temperature control systems. They differ only in worktable structure: HFQ-02 adopts a **2-station push-pull square worktable**, while HFQ-03 comes with a **3-station rotary disc worktable**.

Both models are equipped with a 200×350mm heating platen, with a maximum processing size of 350×200mm. Custom molds with 1~4 cavities are supported. The temperature is adjustable from 0℃ to 300℃, with optional pneumatic or hydraulic pressurization. The heavy-duty frame weighs 350KG (771.62lbs).

They are suitable for double blister packaging, blister-card sealing, toothbrush, small hardware and toy packaging made of APET, PETG, GAG, PVC and PP. They can also thermally fuse EVA fabric, leather, OPP and PU materials, and match small coil auxiliary lines.
HFQ-02 2-station square table is ideal for R&D sampling and small-batch scattered orders; HFQ-03 3-station rotary disc enables continuous cyclic production by one operator, widely used in sample workshops, small & medium packaging factories and leather processing plants.

## 4. Operation Process
### HFQ-02 Manual 2-Station Square Table Operation Steps
1. Install customized identical molds on two stations. Connect air compressor for pneumatic model or hydraulic circuit for hydraulic model, adjust down stroke and holding time.
2. Set corresponding heating temperature on the control panel based on materials, preheat the machine, test pressing action by empty push-pull movement.
3. Place blister shells, paper cards and leather workpieces into mold positioning slots on the outer idle station.
4. Push the loaded station into the heating zone, the machine automatically presses down for constant-temperature thermal bonding.
5. Pull out the table after sealing to take finished products; feed materials on the other station alternately for cyclic operation.
6. Turn off heating, air source or hydraulic system after production. Remove molds and clean plastic residues after full cooling, maintain slide rails regularly.

### HFQ-03 Manual 3-Station Rotary Disc Operation Steps
1. Install identical molds on all three disc stations. Connect air compressor for pneumatic model or hydraulic circuit for hydraulic model, adjust pressing stroke and holding time.
2. Set target temperature on the temperature panel and preheat. Rotate disc without load to check smooth rotation and pressing performance.
3. Put blisters, paper cards and leather parts into mold positioning grooves on the outer idle station.
4. Rotate the disc manually to move loaded station to the heating area for automatic constant-temperature heat sealing.
5. Rotate disc to unloading position after bonding to take finished goods; feed materials on spare stations for uninterrupted cyclic production.
6. Shut down heating, air supply and hydraulic system after work. Disassemble molds and clean residues after cooling; lubricate disc bearings and heating components periodically.
